My Story

I first learnt to code in Primary 3, when my Co-curricular Activity (CCA) teacher taught us how to use Scratch to develop a simple game. After that session, I developed an interest for computer science and programming, and wanted to keep improving my ability to code in order to develop better programs that can help others.

In Secondary 2, I participated in the Youth Cyber Exploration Programme (YCEP) that the Cyber Security Agency of Singapore (CSA) hosts annually. This competition provided me with an entirely new perspective on cybersecurity, offering a glimpse into the world of hacking and the exhilaration that accompanies engaging in CTF challenges.

First coding project

My first coding project began as an inside joke within a group of friends (known as "Yes But No (YBN)") whom I met in Secondary 1. I decided to turn this into an actual bot after having been inspired by Discord bots such as Dyno and MEE6, and wanted to learn to develop my own.

It was a challenging process, and I ended up relying on the help of some of my older friends, whom were much more versed than I was in programming at the time. Eventually, I managed to get a working prototype running after a few months of trial-and-error , and shared with with the rest of YBN.

First CTF achievement

The first CTF that I won a prize in was the Advanced Youth Cyber Exploration Programme (AYCEP) in 2021. Teaming up with several older students, we managed to win the Team CTF about incident response, and got us featured on the Straits Times article about the CTF. Although I was unable to contribute a significant amount to the team, I managed to learn a great deal about incident response, and the steps that should be taken during incident response.

This was also where I won my hoodie, which I continued to wear up until now, in various settings such as in school, in public and in various other competitions such as YCEP 2022 and 2023.
